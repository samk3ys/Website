EAGLE PROJECT

June 15, 2013

Each Scout must lead a service project as their final milestone on the path to the highest rank of Eagle Scout. My project's goal was to replace a rotting picnic table with a more durable table along with benches surrounding a track and soccer field. Before starting the project I had to fill out a mountain of paperwork, get approval from the surrounding neighborhood, raise funds, and flesh out my plan. Thanks to some generous donations I was able to get the table, 3 benches, plaques commemorating those donors (from Eagle Sign & Design), plus trees and shrubs. To help the actual work go smoothly I designed teams with specific tasks laid out on some graphical flyers. My wonderful friends of Troop 71 divided into those teams and helped bring my project to fruition. The result was better than I hoped and everything is still being enjoyed.

This was my first big project. I worked very hard to ensure it went swimmingly. I accomplished something I believe was important and useful, while learning a lot along the way. My brother completed his Eagle Project during the same year and we both achieved the rank of Eagle Scouts; we the two youngest Eagles our troop had seen yet. We remained active in our troop and ended with 6 palms (additional degrees to the rank of Eagle Scout) each. Scouting taught me countless valuable lessons, brought me many friends, and took me on wilderness adventures. For everything the BSA did I am grateful and I would not be the man I am today without it's institution.
